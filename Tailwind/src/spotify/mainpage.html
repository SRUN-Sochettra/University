Introduction

Heritage is the story of who we are, where we come from, and what we value as human beings. It is not only found in grand monuments or ancient ruins, but also in the traditions, beliefs, languages, and artistic expressions that shape our identities and connect us to our ancestors. Around the world, heritage takes many forms—spectacular temples rising from the jungles, sacred landscapes carved by nature over centuries, or songs and dances passed down from generation to generation.

Through preservation, education, and appreciation, we can ensure that these treasures continue to inspire wonder and pride, long into the future.

Built Heritage

Built heritage includes structures of historical and architectural significance like temples, castles, and ancient cities. These monuments tell stories of past civilizations and cultures. Examples include Angkor Wat in Cambodia, the Great Wall of China, and the Roman Colosseum.



Natural Heritage

Natural heritage includes landscapes, ecosystems, and species of outstanding universal value. These areas help protect biodiversity and maintain ecological balance. Famous examples include the Amazon Rainforest, the Great Barrier Reef, and Cambodia's bird sanctuaries.



Caltural Heritage

Cultural heritage includes the traditions, languages, rituals, music, dances, festivals, and knowledge passed down through generations. It represents the identity and values of communities around the world.

In Cambodia, shadow puppetry, classical dance, and traditional music are vital parts of its cultural heritage. These living traditions help connect people to their roots and promote cultural diversity.



About UNESCO

UNESCO, the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization, was founded in 1945 with a mission to build peace through international cooperation in education, science, and culture. One of its most recognized roles is preserving cultural and natural heritage around the world.

Through the World Heritage program, UNESCO identifies and protects landmarks and traditions of outstanding value to humanity—from ancient cities and monuments to traditions and natural wonders. These sites are not only important to the countries they belong to but also to the world as a whole.

By promoting knowledge, cultural diversity, and sustainable development, UNESCO helps ensure that future generations can continue to learn from and be inspired by the achievements of our past.
